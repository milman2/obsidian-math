# <span class="header-definition">Definition</span>

## Homology Theory^[호몰로지 이론]

**Homology theory^[호몰로지 이론]**는 위상공간에 abelian group^[아벨 군]들의 sequence^[수열]를 대응시켜 위상적 성질을 대수적으로 연구하는 이론이다.

**핵심 아이디어**: "구멍(hole)"을 대수적으로 측정

- $H_0(X)$: Connected components^[연결 성분] 측정
- $H_1(X)$: 1차원 구멍 (loops) 측정
- $H_2(X)$: 2차원 구멍 (cavities) 측정
- $H_n(X)$: $n$차원 구멍 측정

---

## Chain Complex^[사슬 복합체]

**Chain complex^[사슬 복합체]**는 abelian groups와 homomorphisms의 sequence:

$$\cdots \xrightarrow{\partial_{n+2}} C_{n+1} \xrightarrow{\partial_{n+1}} C_n \xrightarrow{\partial_n} C_{n-1} \xrightarrow{\partial_{n-1}} \cdots$$

where $\partial_n \circ \partial_{n+1} = 0$ for all $n$

**표기**:
- $C_n$: $n$-chains^[사슬]
- $\partial_n$: Boundary operator^[경계 연산자]
- $\partial_n \circ \partial_{n+1} = 0$: "Boundary of boundary is zero"

### Cycles and Boundaries

**$n$-cycle^[순환]**: $Z_n = \ker(\partial_n) = \{c \in C_n : \partial_n(c) = 0\}$

**$n$-boundary^[경계]**: $B_n = \text{im}(\partial_{n+1}) = \{\partial_{n+1}(c) : c \in C_{n+1}\}$

**핵심 관계**: $\partial_n \circ \partial_{n+1} = 0 \Rightarrow B_n \subseteq Z_n$

---

## Homology Groups^[호몰로지 군]

**$n$-th homology group^[제$n$ 호몰로지 군]**:

$$H_n(C) = \frac{Z_n}{B_n} = \frac{\ker(\partial_n)}{\text{im}(\partial_{n+1})}$$

**해석**:
- Elements of $H_n$: Equivalence classes of cycles
- Two cycles are equivalent if their difference is a boundary
- $H_n = 0 \Leftrightarrow$ 모든 cycle이 boundary

**직관**: $H_n$은 "boundary가 아닌 cycle"을 측정

---

## Singular Homology^[특이 호몰로지]

### Singular Simplex

**Standard $n$-simplex^[표준 단체]**:

$$\Delta^n = \left\{(t_0, \ldots, t_n) \in \mathbb{R}^{n+1} : \sum_{i=0}^n t_i = 1, \, t_i \geq 0\right\}$$

- $\Delta^0$: Point (점)
- $\Delta^1$: Line segment (선분)
- $\Delta^2$: Triangle (삼각형)
- $\Delta^3$: Tetrahedron (사면체)

**Singular $n$-simplex^[특이 단체]** in space $X$:

$$\sigma: \Delta^n \to X \quad \text{(continuous map)}$$

### Singular Chain Complex

**Singular $n$-chain group^[특이 사슬 군]**:

$$C_n(X) = \text{Free abelian group generated by singular } n\text{-simplices}$$

Elements: Formal sums $\displaystyle\sum_{i} n_i \sigma_i$ where $n_i \in \mathbb{Z}$, $\sigma_i: \Delta^n \to X$

**Boundary operator^[경계 연산자]**:

$$\partial_n: C_n(X) \to C_{n-1}(X)$$

$$\partial_n(\sigma) = \sum_{i=0}^n (-1)^i \sigma|_{[v_0, \ldots, \hat{v}_i, \ldots, v_n]}$$

where $\hat{v}_i$ means omitting vertex $v_i$

**Example** ($n = 2$):

$$\partial_2(\sigma) = \sigma|_{[v_1, v_2]} - \sigma|_{[v_0, v_2]} + \sigma|_{[v_0, v_1]}$$

### Singular Homology Groups

**정의**:

$$H_n(X) = \frac{\ker(\partial_n: C_n(X) \to C_{n-1}(X))}{\text{im}(\partial_{n+1}: C_{n+1}(X) \to C_n(X))}$$

**Properties**:
- Topological invariant^[위상 불변량]: Homeomorphic spaces have isomorphic homology
- Homotopy invariant^[호모토피 불변량]: Homotopy equivalent spaces have isomorphic homology

---

## Simplicial Homology^[단체 호몰로지]

### Simplicial Complex

**Simplicial complex^[단체 복합체]** $K$: Collection of simplices satisfying:
1. If $\sigma \in K$ and $\tau$ is a face of $\sigma$, then $\tau \in K$
2. If $\sigma, \tau \in K$, then $\sigma \cap \tau$ is a face of both (or empty)

**Geometric realization^[기하 실현]**: $|K| \subseteq \mathbb{R}^n$

### Simplicial Chain Complex

**Simplicial $n$-chain group**:

$$C_n(K) = \text{Free abelian group generated by } n\text{-simplices of } K$$

**Boundary operator**: Same formula as singular case

$$\partial_n([v_0, \ldots, v_n]) = \sum_{i=0}^n (-1)^i [v_0, \ldots, \hat{v}_i, \ldots, v_n]$$

**Simplicial homology**:

$$H_n(K) = \frac{\ker(\partial_n)}{\text{im}(\partial_{n+1})}$$

**정리**: For a simplicial complex $K$:

$$H_n(K) \cong H_n(|K|)$$

(Simplicial homology = Singular homology)

# <span class="header-properties">Properties</span>

## Functoriality^[함자성]

**정리**: Homology is a functor from $\mathbf{Top}$ to $\mathbf{Ab}$

Continuous map $f: X \to Y$ induces homomorphism:

$$f_*: H_n(X) \to H_n(Y)$$

**Properties**:
1. $(g \circ f)_* = g_* \circ f_*$
2. $(\text{id}_X)_* = \text{id}_{H_n(X)}$

**의미**: Homology는 topological information을 algebraic information으로 변환

## Homotopy Invariance^[호모토피 불변성]

**정리**: If $f, g: X \to Y$ are homotopic, then

$$f_* = g_*: H_n(X) \to H_n(Y)$$

**Corollary**: Homotopy equivalent spaces have isomorphic homology groups

$$X \simeq Y \Rightarrow H_n(X) \cong H_n(Y) \text{ for all } n$$

**중요성**: Homology는 homotopy type^[호모토피 타입]의 불변량

## Excision Theorem^[절제 정리]

**정리** (**Excision Theorem**):

Let $X$ be a topological space, $Z \subseteq A \subseteq X$ where $\overline{Z} \subseteq \text{int}(A)$

Then the inclusion $(X \setminus Z, A \setminus Z) \hookrightarrow (X, A)$ induces isomorphism:

$$H_n(X \setminus Z, A \setminus Z) \xrightarrow{\cong} H_n(X, A)$$

**직관**: "작은 부분"을 제거해도 relative homology는 변하지 않음

## Dimension Axiom^[차원 공리]

**정리**: For a point $P$:

$$H_n(P) = \begin{cases}
\mathbb{Z} & \text{if } n = 0 \\
0 & \text{if } n > 0
\end{cases}$$

**의미**: 점은 가장 간단한 공간 - 오직 $H_0$만 non-trivial

## Mayer-Vietoris Sequence^[메이어-비에토리스 수열]

**정리** (**Mayer-Vietoris**):

Let $X = A \cup B$ where $A, B$ are open. There exists a long exact sequence:

$$\cdots \to H_n(A \cap B) \to H_n(A) \oplus H_n(B) \to H_n(X) \xrightarrow{\partial} H_{n-1}(A \cap B) \to \cdots$$

**응용**: 복잡한 공간을 간단한 조각으로 분해하여 homology 계산

# <span class="header-theorem">Theorems</span>

## Hurewicz Theorem^[후레비츠 정리]

**정리** (**Hurewicz**):

For path-connected space $X$:

$$H_1(X) \cong \pi_1(X)^{\text{ab}}$$

where $\pi_1(X)^{\text{ab}}$ is the abelianization^[아벨화] of fundamental group^[기본군]

**Higher version**: If $X$ is $(n-1)$-connected ($\pi_i(X) = 0$ for $i < n$), then

$$H_n(X) \cong \pi_n(X)$$

**의미**: Homology와 homotopy groups의 연결

## Universal Coefficient Theorem^[보편 계수 정리]

**정리** (**Universal Coefficient Theorem for Homology**):

For space $X$ and abelian group $G$, there exists a split exact sequence:

$$0 \to H_n(X) \otimes G \to H_n(X; G) \to \text{Tor}(H_{n-1}(X), G) \to 0$$

**Corollary**: If $H_n(X)$ is free abelian, then

$$H_n(X; G) \cong H_n(X) \otimes G$$

## Künneth Theorem^[퀴네스 정리]

**정리** (**Künneth Formula**):

For spaces $X, Y$, there exists a split exact sequence:

$$0 \to \bigoplus_{i+j=n} H_i(X) \otimes H_j(Y) \to H_n(X \times Y) \to \bigoplus_{i+j=n-1} \text{Tor}(H_i(X), H_j(Y)) \to 0$$

**Simplified version**: If $H_*(X)$ is free abelian:

$$H_n(X \times Y) \cong \bigoplus_{i+j=n} H_i(X) \otimes H_j(Y)$$

## Poincaré Duality^[푸앵카레 쌍대성]

**정리** (**Poincaré Duality**):

For closed orientable $n$-manifold $M$:

$$H_k(M) \cong H^{n-k}(M)$$

**의미**: Homology와 cohomology 사이의 대칭성

# <span class="header-examples">Examples</span>

## Example 1: Homology of Point

$$H_n(\{\text{pt}\}) = \begin{cases}
\mathbb{Z} & n = 0 \\
0 & n > 0
\end{cases}$$

**해석**: 점은 connected component 1개, 구멍 없음

## Example 2: Homology of Circle $S^1$

$$H_n(S^1) = \begin{cases}
\mathbb{Z} & n = 0, 1 \\
0 & n \geq 2
\end{cases}$$

**해석**:
- $H_0(S^1) = \mathbb{Z}$: 1개의 connected component
- $H_1(S^1) = \mathbb{Z}$: 1차원 구멍 1개 (loop)
- $H_n(S^1) = 0$ for $n \geq 2$: Higher dimensional holes 없음

**Generator**: Loop going around the circle once

## Example 3: Homology of Sphere $S^n$

$$H_k(S^n) = \begin{cases}
\mathbb{Z} & k = 0, n \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}$$

**해석**:
- $S^2$: 2차원 구멍 1개 (hollow ball)
- $S^3$: 3차원 구멍 1개

## Example 4: Homology of Torus $T^2 = S^1 \times S^1$

Using Künneth theorem:

$$H_0(T^2) = \mathbb{Z}$$
$$H_1(T^2) = \mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z}$$
$$H_2(T^2) = \mathbb{Z}$$
$$H_n(T^2) = 0 \text{ for } n \geq 3$$

**해석**:
- 1개 component
- 2개의 독립적인 1차원 구멍 (meridian, longitude)
- 1개의 2차원 구멍 (cavity)

## Example 5: Real Projective Space $\mathbb{RP}^n$

$$H_k(\mathbb{RP}^n) = \begin{cases}
\mathbb{Z} & k = 0 \\
\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z} & k \text{ odd}, 0 < k < n \\
\mathbb{Z} & k = n \text{ (if } n \text{ odd)} \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}$$

**특징**: Torsion^[비틀림] 포함 ($\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$)

## Example 6: Wedge Sum $X \vee Y$

**정리**: For pointed spaces with basepoint-connected:

$$H_n(X \vee Y) \cong H_n(X) \oplus H_n(Y) \quad (n \geq 1)$$

$$H_0(X \vee Y) \cong \mathbb{Z}$$

**Example**: $S^1 \vee S^1$ (figure-eight)

$$H_1(S^1 \vee S^1) = \mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z}$$

## Example 7: Klein Bottle

$$H_0(K) = \mathbb{Z}$$
$$H_1(K) = \mathbb{Z} \oplus (\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z})$$
$$H_2(K) = 0$$

**해석**: Non-orientable surface - torsion in $H_1$

## Example 8: Complex Projective Space $\mathbb{CP}^n$

$$H_k(\mathbb{CP}^n) = \begin{cases}
\mathbb{Z} & k = 0, 2, 4, \ldots, 2n \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}$$

**특징**: Only even-dimensional homology

# <span class="header-remark">Remark</span>

## Intuitive Understanding

**"구멍" 측정**:
- $H_0$: Disconnected pieces (0-dimensional "holes")
- $H_1$: Loops, tunnels (1-dimensional holes)
- $H_2$: Voids, cavities (2-dimensional holes)
- $H_n$: $n$-dimensional holes

**Boundary of boundary = 0**:

$$\partial \circ \partial = 0$$

이것이 homology theory의 핵심!

## Homology vs Homotopy

| Homology $H_n$ | Homotopy $\pi_n$ |
|---|---|
| Abelian groups | Non-abelian (for $n=1$) |
| Easier to compute | Harder to compute |
| Less information | More information |
| Functor | Not quite functor |

**관계**: Hurewicz theorem connects them

**일반 원칙**: Homology는 homotopy의 abelianization

## Coefficients^[계수]

**일반화**: $H_n(X; G)$ for any abelian group $G$

**Standard**: $G = \mathbb{Z}$ (integral homology)

**Other examples**:
- $H_n(X; \mathbb{Q})$: Rational homology
- $H_n(X; \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z})$: $\mathbb{Z}/2$-homology
- $H_n(X; \mathbb{R})$: Real homology

**Universal Coefficient Theorem**: Relates different coefficient groups

## Reduced Homology^[축약 호몰로지]

**정의**: For pointed space $(X, x_0)$:

$$\tilde{H}_n(X) = \begin{cases}
H_n(X) & n > 0 \\
\ker(\epsilon: H_0(X) \to \mathbb{Z}) & n = 0
\end{cases}$$

where $\epsilon$ is augmentation map

**Property**: $\tilde{H}_0(\{\text{pt}\}) = 0$

**Relation**: 

$$H_n(X) \cong \begin{cases}
\tilde{H}_n(X) \oplus \mathbb{Z} & n = 0 \\
\tilde{H}_n(X) & n > 0
\end{cases}$$

## Relative Homology^[상대 호몰로지]

**정의**: For pair $(X, A)$ where $A \subseteq X$:

$$H_n(X, A) = H_n(C_*(X)/C_*(A))$$

**Long exact sequence of pair**:

$$\cdots \to H_n(A) \to H_n(X) \to H_n(X, A) \to H_{n-1}(A) \to \cdots$$

**해석**: $H_n(X, A)$ measures homology of $X$ "relative to" $A$

## Computational Methods

### Method 1: Direct Calculation
- Construct chain complex explicitly
- Compute kernels and images
- Take quotient

### Method 2: Mayer-Vietoris
- Decompose space: $X = A \cup B$
- Use long exact sequence
- Solve for $H_*(X)$

### Method 3: CW Complex
- Build space from cells
- Use cellular homology
- Simpler chain complex

### Method 4: Simplicial Homology
- Triangulate space
- Use simplicial chain complex
- Combinatorial calculation

## Applications

### 1. Topology

**Brouwer Fixed Point Theorem**: Every continuous $f: D^n \to D^n$ has a fixed point

**Proof idea**: Use homology of $S^{n-1}$

**Invariance of Domain**: Open sets in $\mathbb{R}^n$ cannot be homeomorphic to open sets in $\mathbb{R}^m$ if $n \neq m$

### 2. Data Analysis

**Topological Data Analysis (TDA)**:
- Persistent homology
- Detecting features in high-dimensional data
- Applications: Biology, neuroscience, materials science

### 3. Robotics

**Configuration spaces**: Homology detects obstacles and holes in configuration space

### 4. Physics

**Topological phases of matter**: Classified by homology/cohomology

**String theory**: Homology of Calabi-Yau manifolds

## Cohomology^[코호몰로지]

**정의**: Dual theory to homology

$$H^n(X) = \text{Hom}(H_n(X), \mathbb{Z})$$

**Additional structure**: Cup product^[컵 곱] $\smile$

$$H^p(X) \times H^q(X) \xrightarrow{\smile} H^{p+q}(X)$$

Makes $H^*(X) = \bigoplus_n H^n(X)$ a ring^[환]!

**Advantage**: Richer algebraic structure

## Historical Development

**Henri Poincaré** (1895-1904):
- Introduced concept of homology
- Poincaré duality
- Foundation of algebraic topology

**Emmy Noether** (1920s):
- Emphasized group-theoretic approach
- Homology as functor

**Samuel Eilenberg & Norman Steenrod** (1940s-1950s):
- Axiomatic approach to homology
- Eilenberg-Steenrod axioms

**Jean-Pierre Serre** (1950s):
- Spectral sequences
- Modern homological algebra

## Eilenberg-Steenrod Axioms

A **homology theory** on pairs of spaces satisfies:

1. **Homotopy**: $f \simeq g \Rightarrow f_* = g_*$

2. **Exactness**: Long exact sequence for pairs

3. **Excision**: Excision theorem holds

4. **Dimension**: $\tilde{H}_n(\{\text{pt}\}) = 0$ for all $n$

**정리**: These axioms uniquely determine singular homology on CW complexes

**Generalized theories**: Relax dimension axiom
- K-theory
- Cobordism theory
